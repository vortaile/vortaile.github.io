## Why “Vortaile”?

Vortaile is a nickname I chose as a small tribute to **Voltaire** (François-Marie Arouet, 1694–1778) was one of the most influential writers of the French Enlightenment. He was known for his sharp wit, relentless criticism of religious intolerance, and his advocacy for civil liberties — especially freedom of speech, freedom of religion, and the separation of church and state.

He wrote across almost every genre (plays, poems, essays, histories, and satire), producing thousands of works and letters throughout his life. One of his most famous works, *Candide*, uses humor to criticize the idea that the world is already “the best of all possible worlds.”

I’ve always enjoyed philosophy, and Voltaire became one of my favorites not because of a single idea, but because of how he moved through the world. His writing was sharp, fast, and unapologetically critical — not to show off, but to challenge comfort, expose hypocrisy, and defend tolerance when it mattered.

That kind of voice stays with you.

## What “Vortaile” means to me

I’m not trying to be Voltaire. But I didn’t pick this name for aesthetics either.

For me, “Vortaile” is a reminder to live with a certain standard.

To stay curious, even when it’s inconvenient.  
To think clearly, even when emotions try to take over.  
To speak honestly, but not carelessly.  
To question ideas without hating the people behind them.  
To stand for something, without turning into someone who only fights.

So yeah, Vortaile is just a nickname.  
But it carries meaning — and it’s the kind I want to grow into.
